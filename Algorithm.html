The "Success Manifold" Architecture

UniPro rejects simple keyword matching. We utilize Differential Geometry to treat students as vectors in a high-dimensional space, projecting them onto a "Success Manifold" to find their optimal career path (Geodesic Distance).

1. Vector Space ($R^n$)

Students & Jobs are vectors. We calculate Cosine Similarity ($\cos \theta$) to find semantic alignment, not just keywords.

2. Manifold Projection

We project students onto the curved surface of "Successful Seniors" to calculate the Geodesic (Shortest) Path to employment.

3. Allocation (Gale-Shapley)

Game Theory ensures market stability, preventing congestion where one student gets all offers.
